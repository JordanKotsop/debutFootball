## Content Strategy Distillation

### Core Insight
Parents and players choose agents based on trust above everything else. Michael Tait's specialized sports law training, FIFA credentials, international presence, and hands-on experience placing North American players in Europe give him a credibility profile that most boutique agents can't match. The landing page needs to make trust tangible.

### Key Findings (Ranked by Conversion Impact)

1. **Trust is the #1 decision factor for both players and parents.** Copy must lead with credibility markers: FIFA license, sports law expertise, real international presence.
2. **Parents are the gatekeepers, especially for U18 players.** Copy must speak directly to parental concerns: safety, education, transparency, welfare.
3. **The overseas pathway is the biggest value proposition.** North American players who go to Europe during their formative years earn 8x more over their careers. An agent who can open those doors is worth everything.
4. **Most families don't understand how the agent process works.** The page should educate, not just sell. Informed families convert better because they can see the difference between Debut and others.
5. **The 2026 World Cup is creating a wave.** Youth participation is up 19.5%. More families than ever are exploring professional pathways for their children. Timing is perfect.

### Audience Language Map

| They Say | They Mean | We Should Say |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| "How do we know we can trust this guy?" | I need proof before I risk my child's career | Show credentials, real background, FIFA license |
| "Will my child be safe overseas?" | I'm terrified of sending my teenager abroad | We handle everything: housing, school, welfare, on-the-ground support |
| "What's the catch? How do agents get paid?" | I've heard agents are shady | Commission-based, transparent fee structure, no upfront costs |
| "Can my kid really make it in Europe?" | Give me an honest answer, not a sales pitch | Honest assessment, clear pathway, Plan B included |
| "We don't know where to start" | The whole process feels overwhelming | We guide families from first conversation through placement |

### Competitive White Space
No agent in the North American market combines: (1) specialized training in international sports law, (2) FIFA licensing, (3) presence in Toronto, Miami, Barcelona, AND London, (4) teaching-level expertise in international football business, and (5) hands-on experience with South American sports law disputes at FIFA/CAS. Michael Tait sits at an intersection that doesn't exist elsewhere. The copy should make this obvious without saying "we're the only ones." NOTE: Michael holds an LLM in International Sports Law. He does not hold a law degree (JD/LLB). Copy must frame his sports law expertise accurately without implying he is a lawyer or has a law degree.

> What We Do

### Service 1: Player Representation

**For players ready to go professional.**

We represent players in contract negotiations with clubs in North America and overseas. Every deal we negotiate is grounded in deep knowledge of international sports law and structured to protect the player's interests across salary, bonuses, image rights, and release clauses.

We earn our fee through commission when a deal gets done. No upfront costs. No hidden charges. If we don't deliver, you don't pay.

### Service 2: Career Planning & Development

**For players building toward the next level.**

Not every player is ready for a professional contract today. For younger athletes and their families, we help map out the right development pathway. That might mean staying in a North American academy, exploring European trial opportunities, or evaluating the college route while keeping professional options open.

We assess each player honestly and build a plan that fits their level, timeline, and goals.

### Service 3: International Placement & Trials

**For players ready to test themselves overseas.**

With offices in Barcelona and London and contacts across multiple European leagues, we facilitate trial opportunities and introductions to clubs in England, Spain, Germany, Portugal, Scandinavia, and beyond.

We don't just arrange the trial. We handle the logistics: visas, housing, travel, and on-the-ground support so the player can focus entirely on performing. For families, we stay in constant communication and manage every detail of the transition.

### Service 4: Contract Review & Sports Law Expertise

**For players and families who want to understand what they're signing.**

Michael's specialized training in international sports law means every contract is reviewed with the rigor and detail that protects players. We explain the terms in plain language, flag anything that doesn't serve the player, and negotiate from a position of knowledge. For complex situations, we work alongside qualified sports lawyers to make sure every angle is covered.

> How It Works

### Step 1: Initial Conversation
We start with a call. No commitment required. Tell us about the player, their goals, and where they are in their development. We'll give an honest assessment of how we might help.

If there's a fit, we evaluate the player's current level, identify realistic opportunities, and build a personalized plan. For younger players, this includes family involvement at every step.

Once we agree to work together, we go to work. That could mean negotiating a contract, arranging European trials, connecting with clubs, or advising on the right next move. We keep you updated throughout.

### Step 4: Ongoing Support
Our job doesn't end when the contract is signed. We support players through transitions, check in regularly, and plan the next chapter of their career. We build relationships that last entire careers, not single deals.

## Copy Notes for Design Team

### Tone & Feel
- Premium but approachable. Think luxury watch brand meets trusted family advisor.
- Navy and gold color palette communicates authority and warmth.
- Photography should feel aspirational but grounded. Real pitches, real stadiums. Avoid stock photo "celebration" shots.
- Michael's headshot should be professional but warm. Jacket, no tie. Approachable confidence.

### Typography Guidance
- Headlines: Bold, clean sans-serif. Confident and modern.
- Body: Readable serif or clean sans-serif. Long copy sections need generous line-height.
- Credentials bar: Smaller, all-caps tracking for authority markers.

### Visual Suggestions
- Hero: Stadium-level grass shot (matching the existing design inspiration) with logo overlay
- Services: Clean icon-based layout (four cards or four columns)
- Journey: Numbered step progression, vertical or horizontal timeline
- Global Reach: Compact, understated layout. Four cities in a single row with subtle icons or dots on a minimal map. Do not over-emphasize.
- Michael's section: Left-aligned photo, right-aligned bio text


### Color Palette (from brand assets)
- **Primary:** Deep Navy (#1a1a4e or similar from the logo)
- **Secondary:** Gold (#c4993f or similar)
- **Accent:** Green-to-Cyan gradient (from the alternate logo version)
- **Text:** White on dark backgrounds, Navy on light backgrounds
